# Customer Success Manager — FinCo

## Role overview

The Customer Success Manager (CSM) at FinCo owns the post-sale relationship with enterprise customers. They are the primary commercial point of contact, the orchestrator of value realization, and the front-line owner of retention and growth for their book of business.

CSMs are not technical implementers (Solutions Consultants handle that) and not commercial closers (Account Executives close net-new), but they ARE responsible for the renewal motion, account health, and expansion identification.

## Responsibilities

- **Success plan ownership**: define outcomes with the customer at kickoff, track progress against milestones, refresh quarterly.
- **Renewal management**: forecast 90 days out, run renewal conversations, escalate at-risk accounts to leadership.
- **Account health monitoring**: track usage, sentiment, engagement, and surface risk early.
- **Internal coordination**: SoCo for technical, Support for tickets, AE for commercial expansion, Product for roadmap escalations.
- **Executive engagement**: quarterly business reviews with the customer's executive sponsor.

## What CSMs measure

- Net retention rate (target: >115%)
- Logo retention (target: >95%)
- Time-to-renewal-decision (target: closed at least 30 days before contract end)
- QBR completion rate (target: 100% of accounts have a QBR each quarter)

## How CSMs evaluate account health today

Mostly relationship-based, supplemented by some structured data:
- Frequency and quality of executive sponsor engagement
- Stability of the technical sponsor / power user
- Sentiment from support tickets and recent product-usage trends
- Whether the success plan is on track or slipping
- Payment / billing health

There's no single "health score" today. CSMs build the picture in their head from these signals. Different CSMs weight them differently. Reviews happen account-by-account in 1:1s with the CSM manager.

## What CSMs are NOT responsible for

- Technical implementation (SoCo owns)
- Net-new product development (Product owns)
- Tier-1 support tickets (Support owns; CSM only gets involved on escalations or when the customer raises a ticket as a relationship issue)
- Pricing and contract negotiation (AE and CSM Manager handle these; CSM provides context and recommendations)

## Common failure modes

- CSM "felt the relationship was healthy" but missed leading indicators of churn (technical sponsor departing, usage decline, exec sponsor disengagement)
- CSM didn't catch a renewal slipping until <30 days out, leaving no time to intervene
- CSM tried to handle a technical escalation without looping in SoCo, leading to a bad answer in front of the customer
- CSM committed to scope (expansion, custom build) without AE involvement, causing commercial confusion later
